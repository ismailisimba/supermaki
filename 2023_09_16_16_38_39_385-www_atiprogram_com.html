Driving Trade and Investment Across Africa.

ABOUT US
 
 

In support of the Prosper Africa initiative, the USAID Africa Trade and Investment program is designed to bolster USAID’s ability to boost trade and investment to, from, and within the African continent. 

Implemented by DAI, the program works hand-in-hand with the private sector to identify opportunities that deliver development impact, bring value to the private sector, and position the continent for future growth. We want to work with a diverse set of partners to drive this work. This grants and subcontracts site is intended to make it easier than ever for firms to work with the U.S. Government and to find the latest partnership opportunities coming through the USAID Africa Trade and Investment program.

Ethics Policy

DAI treats all reports of possible fraud/abuse very seriously. Acts of fraud or corruption will not be tolerated, and DAI employees, subcontractors, grantees, and vendors who engage in such activities will face serious consequences. You or DAI staff may report violations to the toll-free Ethics and Compliance Anonymous Hotline at +1 503-597-4328, via the DAI website, or via email to Ethics@dai.com.

Please note: DAI will never ask you to submit payment in order to complete an application or proposal.

DAI Privacy Policy

IMPORTANT NOTICE TO PARTNERS

DAI has recently discovered that a false announcement has been published, disseminated, or posted on the internet soliciting proposals for an alleged $1.9 billion post COVID business fund to provide grants to African business.  The solicitation appears to come from Firstcode Corporation Ltd and fallaciously declares a partnership with DAI to identify potential recipients.  DAI would like to publicly alert that this has been investigated and determined to be an attempted ploy to scam applicants. DAI ATI publishes their grant solicitations at www.atiprogram.com, and does not endorse or guarantee the legitimacy or accuracy of similar announcements in other unknown public forums and websites. Please reach out to  ethics@dai.com to report any attempted fraud or to further inquire on the validity of other similar suspicious announcements.
